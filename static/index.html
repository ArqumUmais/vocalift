Vocalift

Vocalift is an AI-powered vocal extraction platform that separates the human voice from any musical recording with professional precision. Paste a YouTube link, and within minutes receive a clean, studio-quality acapella file — ready for remixing, sampling, transcription, or archival.

What We Do

At its core, Vocalift performs audio stem separation — a process of decomposing a mixed audio signal into its constituent parts. We isolate the vocal stem and discard everything else: drums, bass, guitars, synthesisers, and any other instrumentation that sits beneath or around the voice.

The result is a pristine vocal track that retains the full emotional nuance and dynamic range of the original performance, without the coloration of background music.

The Technology

Vocalift is built on HTDemucs, Meta AI Research's hybrid transformer model for music source separation. HTDemucs operates simultaneously in the time domain and the frequency domain, allowing it to capture both the fine temporal structure of transient sounds and the harmonic relationships between pitched instruments and voice.

Unlike older approaches that relied on simple spectral masking, HTDemucs was trained on thousands of professionally recorded multi-track sessions, giving it a deep understanding of what a human voice sounds like across a vast range of musical contexts, genres, languages, and recording conditions.

Intended Use

Vocalift is designed for music producers, audio engineers, researchers, educators, archivists, and anyone who needs clean vocal isolation from a mixed recording. Common applications include karaoke preparation, sample clearance analysis, vocal transcription, language learning, and music education.

Please ensure you have the rights to process any audio content you submit. Vocalift does not store or distribute your audio. All processing occurs locally on your machine, and output files are retained only for the duration of your session.

Limitations

No stem separation system is perfect. Results depend heavily on the quality of the source recording. Vocals recorded with heavy reverb, close harmonic doubling, or significant overlap with instruments in the same frequency range may exhibit mild artefacts. Live recordings tend to produce less clean results than studio productions. Maximum supported duration is 30 minutes per submission.
